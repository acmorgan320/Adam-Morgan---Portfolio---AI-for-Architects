AI Professional Development Plan

A–08 · Multi-Tool Strategy

Description

A one-year plan (July 2026–June 2027) for phasing AI tool adoption across an architectural firm, sequenced to follow the real project lifecycle so each tool is learned in the workflow context where it delivers the most value.

Methodology

  • Month 1 — establish baseline task-hour and revision-cycle metrics before adopting anything, so later efficiency claims have a real denominator.
  • Months 1–3 — Finch for generative/parametric massing in schematic design.
  • Months 4–6 — Claude Design for client-facing presentations and design narratives.
  • Months 7–9 — SketchPro for DD/CD documentation automation inside live Revit models, with mandatory licensed-architect review on anything touching code or life safety.
  • Months 10–12 — a custom Gemini Gem fed by Plaud.ai transcripts for meeting minutes, plus Part3 for construction-administration submittal review and revision tracking.

Results

  • Each tool section carries its own adoption-status tracker and milestone-completion tracker for leadership check-ins.
  • Year-end framework rests on three goals: efficiency validated against the Month-1 baseline, documented SOPs once a workflow shows consistent gains across more than one team member, and mandatory human review preserved on anything touching code compliance or contractual CA decisions.

Reflection

Pricing and availability for several tools — Claude Design credits, SketchPro pricing, Gemini Gem and Plaud.ai tier limits — couldn’t be confirmed from public sources and are explicitly flagged to verify before budgeting, rather than estimated.
